# Elevator Pitch — Rekentafel

**Slice:** Product Reframe and Competitive Positioning  
**Brand:** Rekentafel (working name) | Codename: TabSettle  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-26

---

## One-sentence pitch

**Rekentafel lets every guest at the table pay their fair share from one QR code — after the waiter opens the bill — without phone ordering or post-meal Tikkie chaos.**

### Variants by audience

| Audience | One-sentence variant |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Restaurant owner** | Rekentafel shortens your last ten minutes per table by letting guests split and pay the open bill from their phones while your waiters stay in charge of ordering. |
| **Guest** | Scan the table QR when you're ready to leave, claim what you ate, tip yourself, and pay with iDEAL — no app, no chasing friends for Tikkies. |
| **Waiter** | One tap opens payment mode; guests handle their own splits on their phones; you close the table when the balance hits zero. |
| **Investor** | We're payment infrastructure for European table-service restaurants — Mollie-native split settlements without replacing waiters or rebuilding POS. |

---

## Thirty-second pitch (spoken, ~75 words)

You know the scene: one person pays the whole bill, then spends fifteen minutes sending Tikkies while the table blocks the next seating.

**Rekentafel** fixes that. Every table has a QR code. Before the meal, it shows the menu and lets guests call the server — **not order from the phone**. When it's time to pay, the waiter opens a payment session. Each guest scans, claims their items or splits shared dishes, adds a tip, and pays with iDEAL through Mollie. The waiter closes the table when it's settled.

We're **not** another QR ordering app. We're split-pay for restaurants that still believe in table service — starting in the Netherlands.

## Sixty-second pitch (investor / partner, optional)

**Problem:** Dutch restaurants lose table turns and guest goodwill to one-payer-plus-Tikkie reconciliation. Full QR ordering fixes payment but kills waiter-led hospitality.

**Solution:** Rekentafel — persistent table QR, waiter-activated payment sessions, collaborative itemized splits, Mollie checkout.

**Traction target (MVP):** One pilot venue, manual bill entry, measurable turn-time reduction on 4+ tops.

**Business model (TBD):** SaaS per venue and/or payment bps; MVP pilot free.

**Moat path:** Session + split logic + NL payment UX; later POS sync and opt-in loyalty — not e-money wallets day one.

**Ask:** Pilot restaurant intro in Amsterdam/Utrecht; Mollie partnership for onboarding kit.

## Pitch guardrails (what to say / not say)

| Say | Do not say |
|-----|------------|
| "Waiter opens payment when you're ready" | "Scan to see your live bill anytime" (hijack risk) |
| "Split by item, equal, or custom" | "Everyone orders from the QR" |
| "iDEAL via Mollie" | "Crypto checkout" (post-MVP, separate rail) |
| "Optional account for visit history" | "Wallet balance" / "stored credit" (regulatory) |
| "Faster table turns" | "Replace your POS" |

---

## Objection handlers (one line each)

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already use Tikkie" | Tikkie works after you leave; we settle at the table with the real bill. |
| "We don't want phone ordering" | Neither do we — waiters still take every order. |
| "Our POS does pay-at-table" | Can six guests pay in parallel on their own phones without passing a terminal? |
| "Guests won't scan another QR" | No app install — one scan, iDEAL, done. |
| "What if someone not at our table scans?" | Bill only opens when your waiter activates the session. |
